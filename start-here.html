Hard line: No fake promises.

What “life hit” usually looks like

Job loss. Medical bills. A divorce. Housing disruption. Caregiving. A month that became a year. None of that makes you irresponsible. It makes you human. Credit just records the impact like a blunt instrument.

Your next steps (simple, repeatable)

Step 1: Get the facts

Before you “fix” anything, know what’s actually on your reports. Not vibes, not fear, not guesses.

Goal: identify late payments, collections, utilization, and errors.

Step 2: Stop the bleeding

Missed payments and high utilization do the most damage, fastest. Stabilize the basics first.

Goal: autopay minimums where possible, reduce utilization, avoid new hard pulls unless necessary.

Step 3: Build forward

You rebuild credit by stacking boring wins: on-time history, controlled usage, and time.

Goal: a plan you can keep for 90 days without white-knuckling it.

If you’re rebuilding

  • Prioritize on-time payment history. It’s the spine.
  • Keep utilization controlled (low is calm).
  • Choose tools that help you build, not traps that profit from your stress.

This site will recommend tools over time. If a tool is risky, we’ll say it. If it doesn’t fit your situation, we’ll say that too.

If your credit report looks wrong

  • Errors happen. Especially after moves, breakups, medical events, or identity issues.
  • Document everything. Dates, balances, letters, screenshots.
  • Dispute carefully. Not emotional. Specific.
